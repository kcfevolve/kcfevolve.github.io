Sweden and are a Swedish tax resident, buy the U.S.-listed SPY ETF

If you live in Sweden and are a Swedish tax resident, it's generally not recommended to buy the U.S.-listed SPY ETF directly. There are two main reasons:

  1. PRIIPs regulations: Most retail investors in Europe cannot buy U.S.-domiciled ETFs such as SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust because they do not provide the required Key Information Document (KID).
  2. U.S. estate tax: Holding U.S.-domiciled ETFs may expose non-U.S. investors to U.S. estate tax if the value of their U.S. assets exceeds certain thresholds.

Instead, most investors in Sweden choose UCITS ETFs, which are European-domiciled funds that track the same S&P 500 Index while being more tax- and regulation-friendly.

Here are some of the most popular options:

Vanguard S&P 500 UCITS ETF | VUAA | 0.07% | Vanguard's accumulating version. SPDR S&P 500 UCITS ETF | SPYL | 0.03% | Very low expense ratio. Invesco S&P 500 UCITS ETF | SPXS | ~0.05% | Another low-cost alternative.

If you're investing through a Swedish broker such as Avanza or Nordnet, you should have access to these UCITS ETFs.

My recommendation

If you're investing for the long term (10+ years) and don't need dividend income, I'd suggest one of these:

  • VUAA – Vanguard, accumulating
  • SXR8 / CSPX – iShares, accumulating
  • SPYL – Lowest expense ratio among the major S&P 500 UCITS ETFs

Accumulating ETFs automatically reinvest dividends, which can improve long-term compounding.

If you're using an ISK account

Many investors in Sweden hold these ETFs inside an ISK (Investeringssparkonto) because:

  • You don't need to calculate capital gains tax on every sale.
  • The tax reporting is simple.
  • It's generally very efficient for long-term investing.

An ISK account combined with a low-cost UCITS S&P 500 ETF (such as VUAA or SXR8) is a common long-term investment strategy for Swedish residents.
