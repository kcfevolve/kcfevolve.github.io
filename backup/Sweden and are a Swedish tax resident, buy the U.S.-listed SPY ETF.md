<p data-start="0" data-end="157" class="PDq2pG_selectionAnchorContainer">If you live in Sweden and are a Swedish tax resident, it's generally <strong data-start="69" data-end="119">not recommended to buy the U.S.-listed SPY ETF</strong> directly. There are two main reasons:<span aria-hidden="true" class="PDq2pG_selectionAnchor"></span></p>
<ol data-start="159" data-end="515">
<li data-section-id="zsh2ix" data-start="159" data-end="352">
<strong data-start="162" data-end="184">PRIIPs regulations</strong>: Most retail investors in Europe cannot buy U.S.-domiciled ETFs such as SPDR S&amp;P 500 ETF Trust because they do not provide the required Key Information Document (KID).
</li>
<li data-section-id="61hjb2" data-start="353" data-end="515">
<strong data-start="356" data-end="375">U.S. estate tax</strong>: Holding U.S.-domiciled ETFs may expose non-U.S. investors to U.S. estate tax if the value of their U.S. assets exceeds certain thresholds.
</li>
</ol>
<p data-start="517" data-end="689">Instead, most investors in Sweden choose <strong data-start="558" data-end="572">UCITS ETFs</strong>, which are European-domiciled funds that track the same S&amp;P 500 Index while being more tax- and regulation-friendly.</p>

<h3 data-section-id="1pkm4f7" data-start="1710" data-end="1744">If you're using an ISK account</h3>
<p data-start="1746" data-end="1837">Many investors in Sweden hold these ETFs inside an <strong data-start="1797" data-end="1828">ISK (Investeringssparkonto)</strong> because:</p>
<ul data-start="1838" data-end="1988">
<li data-section-id="n80jss" data-start="1838" data-end="1900">
You don't need to calculate capital gains tax on every sale.
</li>
<li data-section-id="1nc92x4" data-start="1901" data-end="1931">
The tax reporting is simple.
</li>
<li data-section-id="1cde1t8" data-start="1932" data-end="1988">
It's generally very efficient for long-term investing.
</li>
</ul>
<p data-start="1990" data-end="2139" data-is-last-node="" data-is-only-node="">An <strong data-start="1993" data-end="2051">ISK account combined with a low-cost UCITS S&amp;P 500 ETF</strong> (such as VUAA or SXR8) is a common long-term investment strategy for Swedish residents.</p>
